# Kalakeri Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Kalakeri Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Ashwagangha Mountains | The forbidden ruins of the accursed city of Bahru lie hidden somewhere in the Ashwagangha Mountains. A powerful evil force lies imprisoned there, attended by monstrous cultists. |
| Bay of Lost Lights | The villages of Kattachira and Grantha flank the Bay of Lost Lights, which is named for the [will-o'-wisps](/bestiary/undead/will-o-wisp-xmm.md) that bob in and above it. |
| Drowned Altar | The sea boils around an ancient, submerged ruin called the Drowned Altar. With Kalakeri's upheaval, no one has performed the rites to placate what dwells in the deep. |
| Forest of the Still | Statues of helpless animals and terrified refugees alike decorate the quiet, [basilisk](/bestiary/monstrosity/basilisk-xmm.md)-infested Forest of the Still. Some of the statues are said to be the remains of heroes from past ages. |
| Gharial Rivers | The villages of Banyan and Veena are renowned for their remarkable herbalists, but their secrets rely on plants guarded by the titanic crocodiles of the Gharial Rivers. |
| Island of the Naga | The eternal [guardian nagas](/bestiary/celestial/guardian-naga-xmm.md) of the Isle of the Naga have a perfect memory of Kalakeri's lost secrets. Suspicious of strangers, the nagas test the worthiness of any who seek their knowledge. |
| Jalendu's Sanctuary | The [stone giant](/bestiary/giant/stone-giant-xmm.md) guru Jalendu lives in his sanctuary and claims to know a path to religious enlightenment. Those who fail to prove their devotion to his teachings turn up petrified. |
| Meenakara | The shadows of people of Meenakara are disappearing, and the shadow-bereft soon sicken and die. Meenakara's people believe the cure lies in the Ashram of Niranjan. |
| Neelakurinji | Bandits overtook the village of Neelakurinji, claiming Arijani Vasavadan as their leader. In truth, they serve no one, opportunistically robbing those who fear disobeying the Vasavadan factions. |
| Silent Sitars | The giants who occupy the islands known as the Silent Sitars were cursed for offending the gods. Sound is magically suppressed on these isles, stifling the truths within the giants' music. |
| The Vochalams | Temples crown the Vochalam mountains. In one, a deity placed a treasure that grants wishes. In the other is a treasure that turns people into wyverns loyal to Kalakeri's ruler. |
