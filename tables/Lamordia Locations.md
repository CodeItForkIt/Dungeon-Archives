# Lamordia Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Lamordia Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Chokingmere | Lamordia's ancient druidic peoples buried their dead in the Chokingmere. Prospectors seeking pockets of rare gas in this swamp disturb forgotten ritual sites and bog mummy burial sites. |
| Forest of Rust | Ludendorf dumps its waste and scrap metal in the Forest of Rust. Creatures of living iron, [Rust Monsters](/bestiary/monstrosity/rust-monster-xmm.md), and hungry beasts prowl the wooded junk heaps. |
| Isles of Agony | Lamordians know the Isles of Agony as freezing death traps. At the hidden sanctuary of Hope's Heart, Mordenheim's runaway creation, Elise, shelters all manner of outcasts. |
| Lorelei Falls | The waters of the Boiling River are poisoned by mining. Those who listen at Lorelei Falls claim to hear screaming, as though the water were burning someone behind the falls. |
| Schloss Aubrecker | Baron Rudolph von Aubrecker, the reclusive ruler of Lamordia, sends proclamations to his people from the remote castle Schloss Aubrecker to hide the fact that he's a [Brain in a Jar](/bestiary/undead/brain-in-a-jar-rhw.md) (see chapter 5). |
| Schloss Cutter | A former academic, Shoshan Cutter isolates her family at her ancestral home, Schloss Cutter. She supports any who fight against greed and amorality cloaked as innovation. |
| Schloss Mordenheim | Dr. Viktra Mordenheim's home, Schloss Mordenheim, is an imposing castle that hosts the doctor's laboratory and servitor creations. She frequently needs new lab assistants. |
| The Sleeping Beast | Radioactive minerals vein this jagged mountain range. Legends claim the range is a single titanic, sleeping monster and that its blood and bones are the source of rare minerals. |
| Tallman's Wood | Tallman's Wood is the storied home of Tallman, a being that "wears" a too-tall, humanlike body. Tallman kidnaps those who sleep in its wood but also knows much of other lands. |
| Whale End | A theater overlooks the fishing village of Whale End. Concrete encloses every entrance to the theater, and the sealed main entry bears a message: "Forgive me, all those I love." |
| Winter's Mouth | Tales say the sea answers questions posed to it through a fissure in the ice called Winter's Mouth. Those who fall into the waters here return plagued by visions of the drowned. |
