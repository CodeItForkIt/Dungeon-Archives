# Mordent Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Mordent Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Abbey Point | A group of silent Ezra worshippers maintain a sanctuary at Abbey Point. Maps there claim that only the sea lies north of the abbey, making the nearby wooded coast a mystery. |
| Blairsbay | Little more than a dock, market, and tavern, Blairsbay serves a line of coastal noble estates. Many of these great homes are abandoned, including Lumley House and Preston Hill. |
| Candle Cross | The hamlet of Candle Cross spills from the remains of a ruined castle, the drawbridge of which extends over the Arden River. The bridge rises of its own accord to bar evil from entering. |
| Crawford | Near the village of Crawford, countless of ravens roost at Crawford Manor. A carving in the manor reads: "The Death of Ravens Shalt Thou Abhor, Lest the Crawford Line Be Nevermore." |
| Glaston | The residents of the village of Glaston cleave to druidic traditions. Rumors say ancient druids forged magical passages between the nearby Sigil Lakes and similar sites in realms beyond. |
| Glenwich | Years ago, the sand cliff that bore half the village of Glenwich—including its ancient church—collapsed into the bay. Locals avoid the tide pools mingling amid dilapidation and the dead. |
| Haworth Hall | Haworth Hall numbers among the domain's dozens of lonely, decaying manors. Something inhabits the estate, but whatever it is terrifies Mordent's ghost and they avoid it at all costs. |
| Idlethorp | Miniature constructs made of stitched-together body parts run amok in this abandoned hamlet's Punchinel Manor, once home to an eccentric inventor. |
| Locustbranch Hospital | Locustbranch was once a renowned hospital, but it was abandoned after a series of mysterious deaths. Now, ghostly patients pack its wards, crying out for cures to eternal ills. |
| Waterford | The hamlet of Waterford stands at a confluence of rivers and roads. Here, the Globe inn quietly keeps a back entrance and rooms to accommodate silent, ghostly travelers. |
| Whelk Spring House | The last of the noble Esker family recently died, bequeathing Whelk Spring House to distant relatives. At low tide, the waters of Bent Key Causeway reveal a muddy path to the estate. |
