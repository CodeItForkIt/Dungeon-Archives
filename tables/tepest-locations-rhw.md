# Tepest Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Tepest Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Arkenheights | Mysterious valleys hide amid the forested Arkenheights mountains. Each is home to territorial fey who know the strange histories and mystical properties that linger in each. |
| Blackroot | A gigantic evil [Treant](/bestiary/plant/treant-xmm.md) called Blackroot roots amid the stone circles of an ancient order of druids. Blackroot hoards the druids' magic and protects its glade with its deadly saplings. |
| Briggdarrow | This ruined village of Briggdarrow is overgrown by a toxic, vision-inducing fungus. Eerie fungal beings emerge from the ruins by night to spread the infestation beyond the village. |
| Cas Island | A ruined fortress covers most of Cas Island. Within it lies a mysterious magical relic, but the waters around the island are guarded by a notorious, crocodilian lake monster, the Avanc. |
| The Cauldron | A fey witch drowned in this pool long ago. Now, any corpse bathed in its magical waters returns to life—but the spirit inhabiting the body never matches the original owner's. |
| Goblinwood | People-eating goblins lurk in the Goblinwood. They attack trespassers, but a curse affecting the goblins prevents them from seeing anyone wearing a cap of a certain color. |
| Kellee | The walled village of Kellee once worshipped Mother, but something went wrong. Now, Lorinda's forsaken followers haunt the place, and neither she nor her current worshippers dare approach. |
| The Lost Court | By day, this idyllic valley is serene and filled with friendly animals. At night, the ground turns to mud and brambles, from which rise the transformed victims of fey curses. |
| Mount Arak | Beneath Mount Arak lies a hidden realm of the fey. The goodly Queen Maeve and wicked Prince Loht jointly rule their people. The fey resent the surface folk for ancient slights. |
| Nobody's Inn | Fey creatures avoid this sturdy, welcoming Nobody's Inn. Few travel so far from Viktal, but those who do are welcomed by Bryonna, the inn's gregarious but unnerving skeleton proprietor. |
| Seer's Glass | The mountain lake called Seer's Glass has prophetic powers. Those who gaze on their reflection in it see their past by day and their future by night. |
