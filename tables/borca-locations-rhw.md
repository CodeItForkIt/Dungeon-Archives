# Borca Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Borca Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Alichino's Hall | No one knows who built the cursed mansion of Alichino's Hall, but tales claim that devils gather here nightly to gamble. Allegedly anyone who bests the cheating devils is granted a wish. |
| House of Lament | The abandoned House of Lament abuts the tower of a ruined castle. Numerous spirits haunt this accursed place, which has been known to appear in the Mists and in other domains. |
| Lechberg | The residents of Lechburg pretend not to notice that many of their neighbors are clockwork creations of Ivan Dilisnya, fearing their unpredictable lord's reprisal if they don't play along. |
| Leoni | Owned by the Ocrotire family, Leoni has a baseless reputation for harboring bandits. Zealous circuit judges from Sturben often visit and arrest those they deem suspicious. |
| New Ilvin | After Ilvin burned, the residents rebuilt their homes to the east. The people of New Ilvin never speak of the tragedy that befell them and constructed a new road to avoid Old Ilvin. |
| Noble estates | The homes of Borca's most influential families are detailed in "Nobles of Borca." |
| Old Ilvin | Only ruins remain of the town of Ilvin. Hidden within sealed cellars lie the charred remains of many who now appear to reside in the town of New Ilvin. |
| Poison Orchard | Poisonous fungi and plants grow in the Poison Orchard—the deadliest grow at the forest's heart. Several noble-backed companies use these plants as secret ingredients in their products. |
| Scholomance | Scions of Borca's noble families viciously compete to attend the elite Scholomance school of magic. The school is home to the Rainmaker Society, which is said to control Borcan politics. |
| Sturben | Sturben is home to the Faceless Court, in which groups of five masked judges execute severe justice across social classes. Their iron-masked circuit judges travel widely. |
| Vor Ziyden | A hound-like horror appears nightly to terrorize the village of Vor Ziyden. Only one local knows the monster seeks the corpse of its brother, who died near a druidic site in the nearby woods. |
