# Barovia Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Barovia Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Amber Temple | A society of mages constructed the Amber Temple long ago to imprison vestiges of malevolent entities. The mages are long dead; mighty evil forces now obsess over the temple's secrets. |
| Argynvostholt | The fortified Argynvostholt mansion lies in ruins haunted by the restless spirits of a knightly order destroyed by Strahd. A statue of a regal silver dragon stands before the manor's entrance. |
| Balinok Mountains | Barovia spans valleys among the Balinok Mountains. Snow crowns the highest peak, Mount Baratok, while the smaller Mount Ghakis is riddled with deep caves. |
| Castle Ravenloft | The grim castle of Count Strahd von Zarovich looms over the village of Barovia. The haunted castle contains Strahd's supernatural servants and the secrets of the Darklord's origins. |
| Gates of Barovia | Mighty gates flanked by decapitated statues of soldiers stand along the Old Svalich Road. They open mysteriously for travelers but allow only Vistani to leave the domain to the east. |
| Krezk | Above the fortified, wooded village of Krezk rises the Abbey of Saint Markovia. Here, a mysterious abbot tends to patients but, in doing so, leaves them permanently changed. |
| Old Bonegrinder | An ominous windmill called Old Bonegrinder is home to a coven of night hags that create magical pastries. Those who consume the pastries enter a stupor filled with vivid, joyful dreams. |
| Ruins of Berez | Moldering ruins are all that remain of Berez, a village that denied Strahd's will and earned his wrath. Nearby stands a statue of a kneeling peasant clutching a rose. |
| Svalich Woods | Packs of wolves, dire wolves, and werewolves prowl the Svalich Woods, hunting those who oppose Strahd. Strange hermits and witches lurk within the deepest reaches. |
| Tser Pool | The Vistani who camp near the Tser Pool lake are often joined by other wanderers. This is also the home of the seer [Madam Eva](/bestiary/npc/madam-eva-rhw.md) (see chapter 5), who knows much of Strahd and the Mists. |
| Vallaki | The walled town of Vallaki hosts regular festivals to lift the spirits of its despondent inhabitants. Those who don't participate risk being punished as malcontents. |
| Yester Hill | A cult of druids devoted to Strahd conducts rituals amid the circle of boulders atop Yester Hill. The Mists nearby reflect a vision of the count's ancestral home in the distance. |
