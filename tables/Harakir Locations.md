# Har'Akir Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Har'Akir Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Bent Pyramid | This bent, black granite pyramid has no interior or obvious purpose. Some Akirrans claim it holds the magic of a forgotten god that one can manipulate using ancient music. |
| City of the Dead | Massive statues and the ornate tombs of heroes and priests compose the City of the Dead. These crypts climb toward Ankhtepot's mountain-size golden pyramid, Pharaoh's Rest. |
| Kharafek's Excavation | The historian Kharafek has discovered numerous tombs at this excavation. Each unleashes a curse once opened, so Kharafek regularly hires new laborers to bear the brunt of these curses. |
| Muhar Oasis | Muhar Oasis supplies the city of Muhar and attracts predators such as [Chimeras](/bestiary/monstrosity/chimera-xmm.md) and [Giant Scorpions](/bestiary/beast/giant-scorpion-xmm.md). During droughts, the water recedes, revealing a lapis lazuli tomb along the banks. |
| Ousa's Pyramid | Ousa's Pyramid holds a gate to the afterlife, but only Thute—a jackal-headed Child of Ankhtepot—knows how to enter. Thute's jackal-masked followers seek to sacrifice all strangers. |
| Path of the Asp | Deadly serpents inhabit the canyon known as the Path of the Asp. The spirits of those slain by these snakes can appear to anyone they know and speak ten words before vanishing. |
| Red Oasis | Akirrans say that the gods gave the Red Oasis to animals and that its waters are poison to people. Supernaturally cunning lions hunt in the area and offer sacrifices to a nearby monument. |
| Sands of Sute | Within the desert rage two vast sandstorms: the Breath of the Forgotten and the Breath of the False. The storms impose tests that punish those whose lives lack consequence or faith. |
| Sek's Tears | People say the waters of Sek's Tears can heal any affliction, but grim Sphinxes guard the oasis. The answers to the sphinxes' riddles are usually found in other domains. |
| Senmet's Tomb | The Child of Ankhtepot named Senmet seeks to steal the pharaoh's throne. From his tomb, Senmet navigates the Labyrinth and stokes rebellion among the living and the dead. |
| White Oasis | The White Oasis is considered holy. A nearby abandoned quarry called River's Shelter houses numerous cursed and dying hermits who seek to cleanse themselves in the oasis. |
