# Valachan Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Valachan Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Council of the Whip Spider | Within this cavern, a sapient [Giant Spider](/bestiary/beast/giant-spider-xmm.md) presides over representatives of countless insect species. Those seeking insect allies might entreat these councilors for aid. |
| Forest of Fossils | Dinosaurs and megafauna seem common in the Forest of Fossils. The region's most notorious predator is Mother Heartless, a heavily scarred [Tyrannosaurus Rex](/bestiary/beast/tyrannosaurus-rex-xmm.md). |
| Gasping Way | The steep walls of the forested ravine called the Gasping Way keep the forest below in near-constant shadow. [Quicksand](Quicksand%20%20%20pit%20xdmg.md), common along this route, acts with deadly swiftness. |
| Poison's Taste | People say the lake called Poison's Taste is filled with the venom of a giant, sleeping snake. Most who drink from the lake die—but those who survive become immune to nearly all poisons. |
| Sangui Cove | Sharks, [Plesiosaurs](/bestiary/beast/plesiosaurus-xmm.md), giant aquatic snakes, and other waterborne menaces battle each other constantly in Sangui Cove. Deep below lie the wrecks of ships, some laden with fantastic wealth. |
| Shuaran | The human warriors of the village of Shuaran value honor above all. They avoid Chakuna and might help her foes or those who offer aid against the deadly howling [Apes](/bestiary/beast/ape-xmm.md) that dwell nearby. |
| Sleepless Forest | Noisy animals and pests are common in the Sleepless Forest. Anything that dies here reanimates as a [Zombie](/bestiary/undead/zombie-xmm.md) the following night, shambles away, and collapses in a quieter resting place. |
| Starving Water | Skeleton-covered ruins jut from the center of the Starving Water. The deadly waters frequently release gases in limnic eruptions capable of suffocating nearby creatures. |
| Throne of Eels | An [Aboleth](/bestiary/aberration/aboleth-xmm.md) named Apez-Zos lurks within bizarre ruins beneath the lake called the Throne of Eels. It has enthralled nearby lizardfolk communities into serving it and seeks to supplant Chakuna. |
| Weeping Rainforest | The predators in the Weeping Rainforest have a sadistic streak, grievously wounding their prey, then leaving it to struggle in high trees. The cries of the wounded ring through the canopy. |
| Yaguara's Heart | Within the canyon of Yaguara's Heart lies a temple, beneath which sprawl blood-painted caverns. Hidden inside is Chakuna's beating heart, which binds her to the land. |
