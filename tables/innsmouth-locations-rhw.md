# Innsmouth Locations
*Source: RHW* 

**Innsmouth Locations**

| Location | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Blind Observatory | Within a ring of bismuth monoliths called the Blind Observatory, the clouds vanish, revealing whirling galaxies. The Cult of the Heavenly Doom searches this yawning infinity for meaning. |
| Cleft of Dreams | Vividly colored gases escape the mouth of the sea cave called the Cleft of Dreams, which extends into surreal realms within the Vaults. |
| Devil Reef | Sahuagin dwell amid caves of sharp, crimson coral in the Devil Reef. They are violently divided between followers of the shark-god Sekolah and members of the Cult of the Scaly Abyss. |
| Drowned Colossus | The head of a giant statue depicting a half-melted, eight-eyed humanoid rises just above the waterline. Its mouth—large enough to sail a small ship through—leads to the Vaults. |
| Eyes of Olhydra | The swampy islands known as the Eyes of Olhydra almost disappear at high tide. At low tide, servants of Olhydra flock to a monument called the Fathomless Gate. |
| Graven Peak | The domain's highest mount, Graven Peak, is carved into the shape of a terrifying octopoid fiend. Its brow is etched with a mysterious, branch-like rune. Deep below lies Cthulhu's Vault. |
| Map of the Eclipse | Hollows in a series of standing stones depict the progression of a crumbling constellation. Cultists believe that a force capable of moving the stars left this message. |
| March of Elders | A procession of thirty-foot-tall bloodstone statues of gaunt, distorted figures circles the ruins of a domed structure. Some of the statues are partially buried in the nearby mountain. |
| Night Light | A tower blazing with a vaporous orange flame resembles a lighthouse, but the island's cultists say the Night Light is meant to draw something close rather than warn viewers away. |
| Phase of Uldun-Dar | These physics-defying ruins change every time they're observed, reflecting wildly different ages and cultures. Sapient, hyperdimensional cats occasionally live here. |
| The Wharf | A ghoul-haunted hotel called the Wharf leans precariously toward the sea. |
| Wrecks of the Shrike | The wreck of the Shrike lies beneath the waves, inexplicably encased in black ice. Nearby, a duplicate of the ship shows evidence of a great fire, and another duplicate lies crushed. |
